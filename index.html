English

Join us for an unforgettable night of conscious reggae music and community solidarity! This fundraising event brings together the power of music and activism to support our brothers and sisters facing legal battles for standing up for justice.

The Roots & Culture

"Chant Down Babylon" is a powerful Rastafarian concept deeply rooted in reggae culture and the teachings of Haile Selassie I. The phrase calls for the spiritual and peaceful resistance against oppressive systems - what Rastas call "Babylon."

In Rastafarian philosophy, Babylon represents all forms of oppression: corrupt governments, unjust legal systems, and institutions that perpetuate inequality. To "chant down" means to use the power of word, sound, and music to weaken and ultimately overcome these forces of oppression.

Bob Marley immortalized this concept in his music, using reggae as a vehicle for revolution. Through conscious lyrics and the hypnotic one-drop rhythm, reggae became the soundtrack of resistance for oppressed people worldwide.

Tonight, we continue this tradition. As we gather to support activists facing legal persecution, we embody the spirit of "chanting down Babylon" - using our collective voice, our music, and our solidarity to challenge injustice and support those who dare to stand against oppression.
